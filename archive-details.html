Report Date: January 20, 2025

Executive Summary

This report summarizes the counseling sessions conducted with the plaintiff regarding their application to convert from Islam. Over the course of 3 completed sessions, significant progress has been observed in the plaintiff's understanding of Islamic teachings.

Session Summaries

Session 1: Plaintiff menyatakan kekurangan pendedahan kepada ajaran Islam semasa kecil sebagai faktor utama. Beliau merasa lebih diterima dalam komuniti Kristian melalui pengaruh rakan sekerja.

  • Kurang didikan agama dari keluarga
  • Pengaruh rakan sekerja yang beragama Kristian
  • Perasaan diterima dalam komuniti gereja

Session 2: Sesi fokus kepada pengenalan konsep kasih sayang dalam Islam. Plaintiff menunjukkan minat dan keterbukaan untuk memahami.

Session 3: Plaintiff mula menunjukkan tanda-tanda refleksi dan pertimbangan semula. Beliau menyatakan beberapa persoalan yang menunjukkan minat untuk memahami Islam dengan lebih mendalam.

Key Observations

  • Plaintiff shows willingness to learn about Islamic teachings
  • Lack of proper Islamic education during childhood identified as main factor
  • Positive response to discussions about mercy and compassion in Islam
  • Beginning to question initial decision to convert

Recommendations

  • Continue with remaining counseling sessions
  • Involve family members or Muslim community for support
  • Focus on practical aspects of Islamic faith in daily life
  • Provide resources for self-study on Islamic teachings

Conclusion

The counseling process is progressing positively. The plaintiff has shown openness to understanding Islam better and has begun to reflect on their initial decision. It is recommended to continue the remaining sessions with focus on building a supportive community connection.
